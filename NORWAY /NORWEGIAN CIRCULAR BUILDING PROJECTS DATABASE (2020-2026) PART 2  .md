**Size:** 8,730 m², 8-story office building  
**Original Building:** 1950 modernist office (former Norwegian Employers' Association headquarters)  
**Heritage Status:** Street facade (Solvåg granite with teak window frames) is heritage-listed

### Circularity Qualification \- WHY THIS PROJECT IS INCLUDED

KA23 achieved 50% material reuse by weight in Norway's first circular retrofit of a heritage-protected building. The project carefully retained and refurbished teak-paneled interior walls, marble finishes, and mid-century elements while actively sourcing salvaged materials from other Oslo demolitions (glass partitions, doors, light fixtures, second-hand steel). The heritage-listed granite facade was carefully disassembled in sections and rebuilt to allow floorplate extension, demonstrating that protected structures can achieve high-performance reuse without typical gutting.

**Primary Circular Strategies:**

1. **Heritage Preservation \+ Circular Retrofit:** 50% reuse target in protected building  
2. **Multi-Source Salvage:** Components from demolition projects around Norway  
3. **In-Situ Preservation:** Mid-century teak panels, marble, original features retained  
4. **Design for Future Reuse:** New materials specified with disassembly principles

## PROJECT 20: PROSJEKT FØNIKS (Damsgårdveien 127, Bergen)

**Country:** Norway  
**Location:** Bergen (Damsgårdveien 127\)  
**Building Type:** Office  
**Status:** Under construction, planned completion 2025-2026

### Basic Information

**Client/Developer:** Frydenbø Eiendom  
**Architect:** Artec AS  
**Planned Completion:** 2025-2026  
**Size:** 2,200 m² (8-story office from 1969\)  
**Original Building:** Frydenbø's old headquarters (1969 office building)  
**Ambition:** Near 100% material reuse ("verdensrekord i ombruk" \- world record in reuse)

### Circularity Qualification \- WHY THIS PROJECT IS INCLUDED

Føniks ("Phoenix") pursues unprecedented near-100% material reuse in office renovation, sourcing components from across Norway and internationally (elevators from Greece, fixtures from Bergen courthouse, windows from Oslo demolitions). The project achieved preliminary 89% on FutureBuilt Circularity Index through innovative closed-loop upcycling—concrete floor cutouts from creating new atrium transformed into elevator shaft walls on same site. FutureBuilt Bergen pilot demonstrates that comprehensive material reuse is possible with creativity, networking, and strategic sourcing.

**Primary Circular Strategies:**

1. **Near-Total Reuse:** \~90-100% target using existing and sourced materials  
2. **Closed-Loop Upcycling:** Floor cutouts → elevator shaft walls (on-site waste transformation)  
3. **International Sourcing:** Elevators from Greece, materials from multiple countries  
4. **Network-Based Procurement:** Loopfront platform and industry contacts

- **Source:** Created during construction when cutting openings for new atrium  
- **Processing:** "Transformeres" (transformed) rather than wasted  
- **Application:** New elevator shaft walls  
- **Significance:** Closed-loop on-site upcycling—building's own waste becomes building material
